Tuesday evening’s online meeting began with a report, from some of our correspondents, on the presence on Discord of a server where the “Italian” Communist Left is discussed.

Discord is a *social network* with more that 150 million users that allows instant communication over voice, text, and video. Created to facilitate communication among *gamers* during *online* gaming sessions, in the last few years Discord has become a digital space for many communities. It was used by the youth to both communicate and coordinate during the “Gen Z protests”, as the recent riots were labeled by the media.

We have always affirmed the importance of *socials*, and, in fact, we are present on Facebook, X, and Instagram. On the web, we insert our *detector* in order to pick up potential *antiform*[^1] signals. The transformation of historical epochs also produces changes in the approaches, means, and languages tied to the topic of revolution. In a “[double direction](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/13/partito_storico.htm)” published in the periodical n. 13, we talked about the historical party that is forming everywhere around us. In a time of search engines and digital assistants, it's easy to find what you’re looking for; when the environment polarises, attention shifts towards certain content. 

On the morning of 1 May in Turin, the usual union parade was held. A large number of young people participated in the “social section” that headed towards the site of Askatasuna social centre – which was cleared out in December – clashing with the police. We were there in Piazza Castello with the press stand and with a leaflet titled “[Imperialismo con l’acqua alla gola](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/volantini/imperialismo-acqua-alla-gola-volantino.htm)”[^2]. In the afternoon and the next morning we gathered with comrades that arrived from other localities, in view of the next work meeting of 23/24 May. Among the tackled subjects was the *blockchain*, with reference to the article “[Dimenticare Babilonia](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/43/dimenticare_babilonia.htm)”[^3] (*n*+1, n. 43), the becoming of [man-industry](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/19/genesi_uomo_industria.htm) (*n*+1, n. 43), the semi-finished *wargame* articles (*n*+1, n. 50, 51) and the question of revolutionary bifurcation (*n*+1, n. 19).

Returning to the work of mathematician René Thom on the topic of catastrophes, we have observed that, when a historical bifurcation is reached, going “right” or “left” doesn’t depend on fate – that is, by an irreducibly random element – but is determined by the entire previous path and by the perimeter (boundary conditions) that led to that event. If history takes a direction, then it is deterministically certain that such a direction was the only possible one. This, however, does not impede the possibility of performing thought experiments (*gedankenexperiment*), as occurs with [uchronia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uchronia) (= without time, like utopia = without place), to imagine how certain historical events could have evolved if another direction was taken (“[Realtà e percezione](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/33/realta_e_percezione_.htm)”[^4], *n*+1, n. 53).

The knowledge of the laws that govern history and  good simulation work can be decisive in taking the right road at *key* moments. The uchronic method is useful, for example, in the study of the Red Army’s advance on Warsaw in 1920 (“[La rivoluzione all’attacco”](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/39/la_rivoluzione_all_attacco.htm)[^5], *n*+1, n. 39), just like the analysis of the “behaviour” adopted by [Occupy Wall Street](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/30/occupy_the_world_together.htm) (*n*+1, n. 30), an organisation that has dissolved, but that could have evolved and transformed into something else. A third event that can be “uchronised” could be a local struggle: that of the delivery riders who, in Turin, on 1 May 2019, are successful at taking head of the union demonstration. After the initial tension caused by their lighting-quick action and the arrival of the police and the union’s security service, a new phase opens where alternate developments may take place: the majority of the demonstrators sympathise with the young workers, who are determined to be heard (see the [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sLQrBAJ7vOM) made by them). After getting over the initial moment of indecision, the idea prevails that it is better to let the delivery riders through and allow them to enter Piazza San Carlo – where a confederal rally[^6] is being held – presenting this decision as their “victory”. All boundary conditions have influenced this type of solution, but what would have happened if the delivery riders had decided not to move, preventing the demonstration from continuing and inviting the locals to take part in the blockade? Uchronia is effective in keeping the brain-muscle trained and getting used to thinking in terms of *wargame*.

In the case of Occupy, we’ve seen the real potentialities in the occupation first of plazas and then of buildings, which did not continue due to state repression and the movement’s shortcomings in strength. In the case of the Red Army’s advance, the occupation of Warsaw would’ve acted as a jumping board towards Germany and, thus, towards Europe’s heart.

In its evolution, our species, precisely due to its intrinsic difficulties and fragilities (neotenic[^7] character), was forced to adopt utensils to survive (labour-hand-brain correlation): now, beyond the stick and the wheel, languages, writing, and math, tools to know and machines to think appear. “Our” theory, that embraces the entire knowledge of our species, is an indispensable tool to know… for the purpose of reversing praxis.

Historically, when polarisation occurs, there is an increase in social tension that leads towards a critical point, common to all revolutions, definable as a cusp or bifurcation: either the defeat of the dominated class, or the definitive disappearance of the old social order. Obviously, the line that runs *on the thread of time* towards the revolutionary rupture is perfectly determined. The intervention of *[someone](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/storici/battilocchio/battilocchio.pdf)* (Bonaparte, Lenin) or *something* (army, party) is part of a determined path, perfectly describable in retrospect. The revolutionary party, as a *product* and a *factor* of history, is an element capable of catalysing the anti-system forces and accelerating the historical course.

In history it’s possible to observe laws that allow us to adopt a doctrine of the modes of production. The temporary nature of capitalism doesn’t derive from the will of groups or individuals, but from its own way of working, as was described in *Capital* and the *Manifesto*. Our deterministic approach is also the result of a historical environment, of a political current, that pushed us to examine topics such as "catastrophe theory”, already described by Amadeo Bordiga years before René Thom.

The *wargame* is an art, but must be backed up by historical and scientific knowledge. Even an [episode](https://www.chicago86.org/lotte-in-corso/italia/piemonte/1235-1d-maggio-rider-le-aziende-sfruttano-il-governo-e-i-sindacati-cianciano-i-rider-lottano) such as the riders’ one, with their bicycles, joining the union demonstration, creating confusion, can be suitable for broad generalisations. After all, “our” fractal structure of revolutions show phenomena of self-similarity despite diversity of scale, therefore a small strike can have similar appearances to a large revolution.

This year, in Turin during the 1 May demonstrations, militants of FIOM[^8] and other political elements have [challenged](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xo97Z6GzGfg) the Partito Democratico section, demanding its exit. Even in this case generalisations could be made and one could notice how, inside the great party of conservation (that spans from institutional parties to union bigwigs), cracks are beginning to show. Turin is a city with a long history of workers' struggles, but today it's a formerly industrial metropolis that has lost more than 250 thousand inhabitants and shows the highest rate of redundancy funds in Italy.

In “social matters” there always exists a critical mass that is liable to changes of state. Society can be treated as a dynamic system or a self-organised criticality, as affirmed by physicist [Mark Buchanan](https://www.quinterna.org/pubblicazioni/rivista/22/una_fisica_della_storia.htm) in his essays (*Nexus*, *Ubiquity*, *The Social Atom*). For example, in the “pile of sand” model, when grains are added the system progressively grows until it reaches a threshold past which its slope can’t increase, and then a collapse occurs. Capitalism keeps on growing but, past a certain point, it’s destined to collapse.

In the social movements over the past years we register a tension towards *something* different from what currently exists, such as the critique to wage labour (“Anti-Work”, “Tǎng Píng”), topic of “[Non-Labour Day](https://www.romatoday.it/zone/centocelle/centocelle/40-anni-forte-prenestino-roma.html)” at the Forte Prenestino community centre in Rome, that has seen the participation of many young people tired of the usual 1 May “Big Concert” in Piazza San Giovanni. That said, there’s still much “old junk” in circulation, from the threadbare language of third-internationalism to demo-reformist illusions, to the resurgent and determined ‘partisan spirit’. Among all the leaflets we’ve gathered during the 1 May demonstration, the only noteworthy one is that of the “movement for paradism”, in which a robot is portrayed as saying: *“Don't be afraid of me, I was conceived to serve, be afraid of my owners. A world without labour and money”*.
